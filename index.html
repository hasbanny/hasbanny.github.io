My name is Hasbanny Irisson, and I am 22-year-old latina living in the Rio Grande Valley. I was born in Veracruz, Mexico and raised in the U.S. I like to be involved in my community, and one of my passions is advocating for victims of sexual assault and domestice violence. In addition, I often look for ways to help women in STEM fields; in fact, a collegue and I have decided to create a website specfically for women in these fields to help them market and promote themselves for job opportunites.

I am a recent graduate from The University of Texas - Rio Grande Valley, where I earned a Bachelors of Science in Computer Science and a minor in Business Management. I have dedicated my last 2 years of college to contributing to important research topics such as, phosphoprotemics analysis, and social interaction in robotics.

My current plans are to continue my education by earning a Masters degree in Computer Science in the same institution. I plan on continuing my research with Dr. Ayati in phosphoprotemics analysis, in which we implement kinase-substrate prediction in cancer to help the medical community develop accurate drugs for this disease.
